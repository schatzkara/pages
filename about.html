Biography

Kara Schatz completed her Bachelor’s degree in Mathematics and Computer Science at Xavier University in 2020 and completed her Master's and Ph.D. in Computer Science at North Carolina State University in 2024. Her disseration was on Domain- and Task-Aware Knowledge Graphs: From Construction to Completion and was completed under the advisement of Dr. Rada Chirkova from NCSU and Dr. Alexander Tropsha from the Eshelman School of Pharmacy at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill. Currently, she is an Assistant Professor in the Computer Science Department at her alma mater: Xavier University. This year, she will teach CSCI 170: Computer Science I, CSCI 180: Computer Science II, and CSCI 335: Databases. Her current research field is data and knowledge management and discovery. In particular, she is focused on developing approaches for discovering new knowledge through pattern mining in large datasets. Currently, she collaborates with biomedical experts at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill and the Renaissance Institute and is applying her work to address drug discovery.
